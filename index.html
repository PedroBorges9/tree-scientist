Method basis

WCC Carbon Assessment Protocol v2.0 section 5 is used for biomass allocation: stem biomass equals estimated total stem volume times nominal specific gravity; crown and root biomass use the protocol DBH equations; tree carbon is biomass times 0.5; CO2e is carbon times 44/12.

Stem volume is estimated here from DBH, height, and an editable form factor because the section 5 equations require total stem volume as an input. Future sequestration foregone is estimated by projecting DBH and height over the assessment period, recalculating WCC biomass/carbon at the projected size, and taking the difference from current stored carbon. Where the Forest Yield handbook provides a matching Appendix 2 top-height increment table, that species table is used for height growth; otherwise species-group fallback growth is used.

Species not explicitly listed in the WCC nominal specific gravity table are mapped to the nearest broadleaf or conifer calculation group and should be reviewed before formal reporting.

Tree Survey Summary workbooks are imported from columns B-F. Girth is converted to DBH using DBH = girth / pi. Stumps and deceased trees are excluded from living-tree WCC biomass totals.

Rainfall interception is estimated as canopy area x annual rainfall x species-group interception factor. Canopy area is calculated from spread as pi x (spread / 2)^2.

Avoided runoff uses the expanded i-Tree-style screening formula by default: rainfall interception x impervious cover fraction. The simple per-tree rate is retained as a fallback option.
